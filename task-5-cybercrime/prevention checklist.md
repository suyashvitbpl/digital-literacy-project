Student digital safety checklist
1. The "PIN is for Giving" Rule (UPI Safety): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a QR code and claims scanning it will "credit" money to your account, it is a scam.
2. Verify the Payee Name (Financial Safety): Before hitting 'Pay' on any UPI app, double-check the verified name that pops up. Scammers often use names like "Customer Care" or "Refund Dept" to hide their real identity.
3. Lockdown Your Socials: Set your Instagram and Facebook profiles to 'Private'. Use the "Lock Profile" feature to ensure only your friends can see your photos and details, preventing "identity cloning."
4. Use Official University Channels: Only download academic materials,hostel notices from the official university portal. Avoid clicking on "PDF" links sent in random WhatsApp groups or Telegram channel that claim to be "leaked" question papers.
5. Enable 2FA on Everything: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication for your Gmail, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp. Even if someone guesses your password, they won't be able to log in without the code on your phone.
6. Beware of "Task-Based" Jobs: Avoid part-time job offers on Telegram or WhatsApp that ask you to "like YouTube videos or social media posts" or "rate hotels" for quick money. These are front-ends for sophisticated money-laundering scams.
7. Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Avoid logging into your bank account or university portal while using free public Wi-Fi (like at railway stations or cafes). If you must use it, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your data.
8. Check Permissions for "Fun" Apps: Before installing third-party photo editors or "AI Avatar" makers, check if they are asking for unnecessary access to your contacts, SMS, or microphone.
9. The "Golden Hour" Reporting: If you are a victim of financial fraud, every minute counts. Report it immediately to freeze the attacker's account.
10. Practice Password Variety: Never use your registration number or birthday as a password. Use a unique passphrase for your primary email—this is the "master key" to all your other accounts.
 

How to Report Cybercrime in India


If you encounter a scam, face online harassment, or lose money to a digital fraudster, use these official government channels immediately:
 Helpline: Dial 1930 (The National Cyber Crime Helpline).
 Web Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file a formal complaint.
 Local Action: You can also visit the nearest "Cyber Cell" at a police station.
